01 / BUSINESS PROBLEM

How many seats should the airline protect for higher-paying passengers?

Airlines face a recurring trade-off: selling too many discounted tickets improves early load factor but may displace late-booking passengers who are willing to pay significantly higher fares. The objective is to define booking limits that maximize expected revenue while controlling empty-seat risk and denied high-value demand.

Maximize expected revenueProtect capacity for higher-yield demand without creating excessive spoilage.
Quantify the trade-offCompare a first-come-first-served baseline with an optimized policy.
Stress-test decisionsUse 30,000 Monte Carlo demand scenarios rather than a single deterministic forecast.
Translate analysis into actionRecommend booking controls and next-step commercial experiments.

Best policy

Cap Saver sales at 94 seats and protect 24 seats for Business demand.

Revenue effect

The policy generates approximately 36.8% more expected revenue per flight than the baseline.

Management implication

Maximizing load factor alone can destroy value when higher-fare demand arrives later in the booking cycle.

05 / RECOMMENDATIONS

Recommended commercial actions

1. Pilot the booking limitsTest the policy on comparable routes and monitor revenue per available seat.
2. Refresh forecasts frequentlyRe-estimate class-level demand by day-of-week, season and booking horizon.
3. Add dynamic pricingCombine seat protection with fare adjustments as remaining capacity and demand signals change.
4. Segment by routeBusiness-heavy and leisure-heavy routes should not use the same protection levels.
5. Incorporate cancellationsExtend the model with no-show probabilities and controlled overbooking.
6. Measure business impactTrack RASK, yield, spill, spoilage and customer displacement during the pilot.

Executive conclusion

The analysis demonstrates that a simple booking-control policy can outperform a volume-first approach. The central strategic insight is that capacity should be allocated by expected marginal value, not merely by booking order.
